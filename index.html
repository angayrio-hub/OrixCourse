05 — Avoid These

5 Beginner Mistakes

These kill most channels before they ever get a chance to grow. Learn them now so you don't repeat them.

01

Picking a Low-CPM Niche for Passion

Passion doesn't pay the bills. If your niche has a $1 CPM, you'll need 10 million views per month to make $10,000. Pick high-CPM niches you can stay consistent with, not ones you "love."

02

Spending Weeks on a Logo Before Publishing

Perfectionism before momentum is the silent killer of channels. Your channel name and thumbnail matter far more than a logo. Publish your first video first.

03

Ignoring the First 30 Seconds

YouTube measures audience retention closely. If viewers leave in the first 30 seconds, your video gets suppressed. Every script needs a strong hook before anything else.

04

Outsourcing Too Early With No Systems

Hiring a $20 editor before you have a clear production brief, style guide, and feedback process is money down the drain. Build the process first, then hand it off.

05

Quitting After 3 Months of "No Views"

The YouTube algorithm rewards channels that have proven audience interest over time. Most successful channels saw near-zero growth for 4–9 months before a breakthrough. Consistency is the strategy.
